# Statistical Validation of Churn Drivers

To validate patterns identified during exploratory analysis, statistical hypothesis testing was performed.

## Tests Conducted

1. Contract Type vs Churn — Chi-square test
2. Monthly Charges vs Churn — Independent Two-Sample T-test
3. Tenure vs Churn — Independent Two-Sample T-test

## Key Findings

• Contract type significantly influences churn behavior (p < 0.001).

• Customers with higher monthly charges are statistically more likely to churn.

• Customers with shorter tenure exhibit significantly higher churn probability.

## Conclusion

The statistical tests confirm that the churn patterns observed during exploratory analysis are not due to random variation. These findings strengthen confidence in the identified churn drivers and support targeted retention strategies focused on contract structure, pricing perception, and early customer lifecycle engagement.
